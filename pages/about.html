Nowadays, climate change is one of the hottest topics of our time and it is discussed worldwide, from online forums to local news outlets! We are witnessing its impact on our skin and the word sustainability is now on everyone's lips.

As our concern and its popularity rise, companies have been trying to align their businesses to the new green values but more and more people are also talking about greenwashing. But what does it mean?

Greenwashing can be defined as “unsubstantiated claims or activities which deceive consumers into believing that a company has a greater positive environmental impact than is true”. source Sometimes businesses are accused of greenwashing because what they say and what they do apparently don’t match perfectly. Among them are fast food chains that are constantly under the media radar.”

The IPCC Special Report on Climate Change and Land states that 34% of all man-made CO2 emissions are generated by the food industry. Among these, fast food companies play a crucial role in the environmental discourse.

As a matter of fact, these companies originated in the United States, the world's second-largest carbon emitter, with its 5 billion tonnes of CO2 (2021). source

The research focuses on the nine most popular fast food chains websites in the US that were selected by the following criteria: annual revenue, total number of stores open world wide, and has at list one sustainability-related section on their websites.

Glossary

The research study objects are the .com domains of fast food companies. In this way, it was possible to avoid results on a geographical basis and to reach a neutral view of the topic.

To analyse the language, it was necessary to create a custom-made green glossary . It features 41 keywords related to different environmental issues and topics, which are strongly connected to the fast food market and are frequently used in today's green narratives. source

After selecting the most fitting keywords, they were searched on each company’s website and the results were then computed to find out the most used ones.



screening

Furthermore, through thorough screening, some keywords were discarded to cleanse the dataset, in order to avoid repetitions and insignificant results. This decision was taken consciously, with the main goal of not damaging the balance of the statistical sample and analysing the topic in a more relevant and in-depth way.

In order to understand why and how these keywords are used on the chosen fast food companies websites, the dataset was enriched by collecting all the sentences in which they appear. It was then possible to better understand the context of each green term through a heeded categorisation, based on specific discriminants: keywords, topic, aim, evidences.

The aim of the archive is to allow users to explore different kinds of green storytelling made by fast food companies nowdays and the common patterns behind them. The users can choose among recipes composed of ingredients which represent the most used discriminants in every sentence.
